This experience enhanced my skills in software development and provided valuable insights into the challenges of integrating technology with urban infrastructure.

Explain a technical challenge you faced and how you solved it?

While working on C++ projects in my second term of my second year, I ran across a big technical obstacle. My Dj program frequently crashed and experienced segmentation faults because I had trouble with memory management and pointer arithmetic. I spent time studying C++ memory management principles, such as dynamic memory allocation and deallocation, in order to get around this. In order to get alternative viewpoints on the problems I was having, I also worked with colleagues and asked internet forums for assistance. I was able to include efficient memory management strategies into my code by steadily increasing my comprehension through testing and debugging. In addition to improving my C++ abilities, this experience taught me the value of persistence and asking for assistance when faced with technical difficulties.

How do you learn new technologies quickly?

during my foundation year, I had to quickly learn new technologies such as python and web development frameworks. I had struggled initially with understanding the syntax and concepts of python programming and i guess programming in general. To overcome this, I adopted a structured learning approach that involved breaking down complex topics into smaller, manageable chunks. I utilized online resources, tutorials, and documentation to gain a solid understanding of the fundamentals. Additionally, I practiced hands-on coding exercises and built small projects to reinforce my learning. I also joined online coding communities and forums where I could ask questions and learn from experienced developers. In conclusion, my ability to learn new technologies quickly stems from a combination of structured learning, hands-on practice, and seeking support from the developer community.
